# AI Use-Case Prioritization Framework

This framework helps teams decide which AI use cases are worth pursuing first.

## 1. Business problem

Questions:
- What real problem is being solved?
- Who experiences it?
- How often does it happen?
- What is the cost of leaving it unsolved?

Strong signals:
- The problem is specific
- The pain is visible
- The impact is meaningful

## 2. AI fit

Questions:
- Is the task suited to summarization, classification, generation, retrieval, or pattern recognition?
- Is the workflow clear enough to improve?
- Can outputs be reviewed by humans?

Strong signals:
- The task is narrow enough to test
- Inputs and outputs are understandable
- Human review is possible

## 3. Value potential

Questions:
- Will this save time, reduce cost, improve quality, or reduce risk?
- Does it affect an important workflow?
- Does it support a strategic goal?

Strong signals:
- The value is measurable
- The affected workflow matters
- The organization cares about the outcome

## 4. Feasibility

Questions:
- Is the data available?
- Are tools and ownership in place?
- Are dependencies manageable?

Strong signals:
- The pilot can start without major blockers
- The team understands the workflow
- Required support is realistic

## 5. Risk and constraints

Questions:
- Are there legal, compliance, privacy, or brand risks?
- Does the use case require a high level of human judgment?
- Are there failure modes that could create serious harm?

Strong signals:
- Risks are known
- Guardrails can be defined
- Human review can contain the downside

## 6. Priority decision

Suggested outcomes:
- Do now
- Pilot carefully
- Revisit later
- Do not pursue
